## What scales, what doesn't

- **Per-run analysis is sub-second into the several-hundred-node range.** Graph build, mapping, attack paths, scoring, and segmentation stay in the low tens of milliseconds even at ~1100 nodes (the topology is hierarchical, so edge count is near-linear and k-shortest paths stay cheap).
- **Betweenness centrality (chokepoints) is the per-run soft spot.** It is O(V·E) and grows from ~14 ms (94 nodes) to ~2 s (1106 nodes) — the dominant per-run cost at scale. Fix when needed: sample-approximate betweenness (`k` pivots) or restrict it to candidate waypoints.
- **`score_architecture` is ~O(V²)** (per-asset shortest-path exposure + downstream BFS) but small in absolute terms (~85 ms at 1106 nodes).
- **`sensitivity()` is the overall bottleneck.** It re-scores the whole model ~81 times, so it inherits scoring's cost ×81 — already >1 s at 281 nodes, hence skipped above a few hundred here. Fix: memoize the graph-derived factors (exposure, downstream) once and vary only the weights across perturbations.
- **Figures don't scale *visually*.** The network diagram renders, but a many-hundred-node spring layout is an unreadable hairball — at scale the briefing text, risk table, and ATT&CK Navigator layer are the usable outputs, not the graph image. An honest tool would switch to per-zone summaries above ~50 nodes.

**Bottom line.** The narrative (rank assets, find IT→OT bypasses, trace paths, map techniques) holds at scale. Two components need a known, local engineering fix before thousands of nodes: **betweenness** (sample-approximate it) and **`sensitivity()`** (memoize graph-derived factors). Neither is a redesign.
